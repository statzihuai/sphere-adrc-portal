How the model works

Account-based, not license-based

Users create a SPHERE account — one login, one credit balance, one billing relationship. No license negotiation, no procurement decision, no IT approval for individuals. All usage draws from the same credit balance; enterprise customers buy a bundle and sign a DPA.

SPHERE holds the Claude API relationship and charges users at retail rates — no BYOK friction. The AI layer earns ~15% base margin from an Anthropic volume discount, with effective margin significantly higher due to caching (see below).

AI margin — how caching works

Anthropic returns a full token breakdown per API call: fresh input tokens ($3.00/1M) and cache-read tokens ($0.30/1M). SPHERE bills the user at retail for the total — fresh + cached — as if no caching occurred. The 10× cost difference on cached tokens is SPHERE's margin, a return on the caching infrastructure investment.

Two components are cached per session: the SAP knowledge base (~3,000 tokens) and the dataset context (varies). Both re-cache at session start if the 5-min TTL has expired. For ADRC, the dataset context is identical across all users — opening the possibility of a globally shared cache that further improves margin.

Key decisions and why

Decision 1
Account model, not license keys
License negotiation is a barrier for individuals and a procurement conversation for enterprises. Account + credit model means anyone can sign up, add a card, and start in minutes. Enterprise just means a bigger credit bundle and a DPA — no custom deal until serious scale.
Decision 2
Charge by cells (rows × columns), not by row or by dataset
Rows alone misses dimensionality — a 1,000-row omics dataset with 50,000 columns is far more work than 1,000 rows × 10 columns. Cells matches actual compute and is intuitive: users know their data dimensions. All columns included pre-QC for simplicity.
Decision 3
Generate ($4/1M) costs more than Evaluate & Certify ($1/1M)
Generation is the core IP and hard computational work. Evaluate & Certify runs on already-generated data. Pricing eval & certify cheaper encourages users to always run the full workflow — if it cost the same as generation, users might skip it.
Decision 4
AI at Claude retail rates — no markup, full transparency
Researchers and developers know Claude's pricing. Matching it exactly builds immediate trust. SPHERE's margin comes from generation and subscriptions — the AI layer is a retention feature, not a profit center. Publishing a side-by-side table removes any suspicion of hidden margin.
Decision 5
No permanent free tier — $10 trial credits instead
A free tier attracts non-buyers and creates support burden. $10 trial credits cover meaningful ADRC exploration (300+ AI queries, small dataset generation) and convert serious evaluators. After trial, card required.
Decision 6
$29/month — priced for individual expense, not procurement
The target user expenses tools like Cursor ($20/mo) and GitHub Copilot ($19/mo) without asking. $29 sits in that range. Goal at this stage is account acquisition and ecosystem building — not margin maximization. Price rises as catalog value grows.
Decision 7
Enforcement via features, not honor system
Can't technically prevent academic users from using the product commercially. But commercial users need things academics don't: private sharing, on-premise deployment, SLA, audit logs, DPA. These gate themselves. A hospital's legal team won't sign off on a free account — the reputational risk far exceeds the license cost.

End-game platform ARR — marketplace economics (Y6–Y8) · Illustrative

Years 1–5 model a SaaS tool: individual users paying subscription and usage fees. From Year 6 onward, SPHERE becomes a two-sided platform. A second revenue layer — marketplace access fees — stacks on top of SaaS ARR as institutional data producers publish and consumers pay for in-place AI access.

Marketplace GMV = total access fees paid by consumers to explore institutional datasets. SPHERE takes 20–25% of GMV. Producers keep the remainder — the revenue-share model that incentivizes more contributions.
